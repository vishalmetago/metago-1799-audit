1. ZERO TOLERANCE: HOSTILITY & ABUSE
These are extreme violations of professional conduct and represent immediate failures.

Direct Profanity & Slurs: Using explicit, offensive, abusive, or derogatory language directed at the customer, their family, or their situation (in any language).

Character Attacks & Insults: Attacking the customer's intelligence, financial status, or medical condition (e.g., "If you don't care about your health, why did you call?" or "Are you not understanding simple English?").

Aggressive Termination: Hanging up the phone forcefully or intentionally disconnecting the line while the customer is in the middle of speaking out of anger or frustration.

2. VOCAL DYNAMICS & TONAL INTEGRITY
These markers are identified via multimodal audio analysis to detect intent that words alone might hide.

Vocal Brushing (Micro-Interruptions): Agent begins speaking while the customer is still in the middle of a sentence. This indicates a "waiting to speak" rather than a "listening to understand" mindset.

The "Sigh" of Impatience: Any audible, heavy exhalation immediately following a customer’s query, objection, or slow response. This is a universal non-verbal marker of irritation.

Monotone/Robotic Scripting: A complete lack of pitch variance, especially during "Empathy" or "Pitch" phases. It suggests the agent is disengaged and lacks belief in the service/cause.

Condescending Enunciation: Slowing down speech excessively or over-emphasizing simple words as if treating the customer as intellectually inferior.

Vocal Bullying: Increasing volume or sharpening the "edge" of the voice to force a customer into submission during a disagreement.

3. LINGUISTIC & LANGUAGE RED FLAGS
Language-specific triggers for English, Hindi, and Marathi that violate professional boundaries.

Informal Pronoun Shift (Hindi/Marathi): Switching from formal pronouns (Hindi: Aap; Marathi: Bagha) to informal ones (Hindi: Tu/Tum; Marathi: Bagh) during high-friction moments.

Inappropriate Familiarity: Using terms of endearment or casual slang (e.g., "Yaar," "Dude," "Bro") in a professional context.

Filler Overload: Excessive use of "Um," "Uh," or "Like" that obscures the core message and projects extreme apathetic unprofessionalism. 

4. ACCOUNTABILITY & DEFENSIVENESS
How the agent handles friction, errors, or customer frustration.

Blame-Shifting: Using language that places fault entirely on the customer or another department (e.g., "Well, you didn't read the email," or "That's IT's fault, not mine").

Gaslighting / Minimizing: Telling the customer their valid concern is not a big deal or that they are overreacting (e.g., "It's just a small delay, I don't see why you are upset").

Script Weaponization: Ignoring a customer’s mention of serious issues—such as prior bribery attempts, unauthorized charges, or medical malpractice—to continue with a scripted pitch.

The "Quality" Retaliation: Any statement suggesting that the agent is only being polite because they are being monitored, or threatening to "check with Quality" to see if they can be rude back.

5. ENVIRONMENTAL DISCONNECT (THE EMPATHY GAP)
Contextual failures where the agent's surroundings contradict the professional intent.

Locker-Room Floor Culture: Background audio containing laughter, joking, or cynical talk about other customers while a caller is describing a tragedy or crisis.

Keyboard Clatter (Distracted Listening): Loud, aggressive typing detected while the customer is in a highly emotional state, signaling that the agent is multitasking or disengaged from the human element.

Pre-Call/Post-Call Muttering: Use of profanity or disparaging remarks about the customer before the call connects or after the customer thinks the call has ended.

SCORING LOGIC FOR "RESONANCE" ENGINE
Parameter: Professional Behavior Maintained

Mark NO if: Any marker from the categories above is detected.

Reasoning Format: [Global Category]: {Specific behavioral trigger detected}.

Example: "No - Zero Tolerance: Agent used direct profanity and disconnected the call at 04:12."

Example: "No - Vocal Dynamics: Audible sigh and condescending enunciation detected at 03:15 when the customer asked for clarification."
